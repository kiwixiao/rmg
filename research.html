Research Publications

Scientific contributions and peer-reviewed research by Dr. Alister Bates

Human upper-airway respiratory airflow: In vivo comparison of computational fluid dynamics simulations and hyperpolarized 129Xe phase contrast MRI velocimetry

Qiwei Xiao, Neil J. Stewart, Matthew M. Willmering, Chamindu C. Gunatilaka, Robert P. Thomen, Andreas Schuh, Guruprasad Krishnamoorthy, Hui Wang, Raouf S. Amin, Charles L. Dumoulin, Jason C. Woods, Alister J. Bates
PLOS ONE • 2021
CFDRespiratory ModelingMRI Validation

Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations of respiratory airflow have the potential to change the clinical assessment of regional airway function in health and disease. This study compared CFD simulations with phase contrast MRI using hyperpolarized 129Xe gas in three healthy subjects, showing good qualitative agreement in flow patterns and statistically significant correlations between velocity values.

DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0256460

The Effect of Airway Motion and Breathing Phase During Imaging on CFD Simulations of Respiratory Airflow

Alister J. Bates, Andreas Schuh, Guruprasad Krishnamoorthy, Robert P. Thomen, Jason C. Woods
Annals of Biomedical Engineering • 2021
CFDDynamic Airway MotionClinical Applications

This study quantifies how much the airway moves during the respiratory cycle and determines how this motion affects CFD pressure loss predictions. The research demonstrates that realistic movement of the airway is vital to producing clinically relevant computational models of respiratory airflow.

DOI: 10.1007/s10439-020-02669-2

A novel method to generate dynamic boundary conditions for airway CFD by mapping upper airway movement with non-rigid registration of dynamic and static MRI

Alister J. Bates, Andreas Schuh, Guruprasad Krishnamoorthy, Robert P. Thomen, Charles L. Dumoulin, Jason C. Woods
International Journal for Numerical Methods in Biomedical Engineering • 2018
CFDDynamic ModelingMRI Registration

A novel method for generating dynamic boundary conditions for CFD simulations by mapping upper airway movement using non-rigid registration of dynamic and static MRI. This approach enables the incorporation of realistic airway motion into CFD models.

DOI: 10.1002/cnm.3144

Assessing the relationship between movement and airflow in the upper airway using computational fluid dynamics with motion determined from magnetic resonance imaging

Alister J. Bates, Andreas Schuh, Guruprasad Krishnamoorthy, Robert P. Thomen, Charles L. Dumoulin, Jason C. Woods
Scientific Reports • 2017
CFDAirway MovementMRI Analysis

This study assesses the relationship between airway wall movement and airflow using computational fluid dynamics with motion determined from magnetic resonance imaging. The work demonstrates the importance of including dynamic wall motion in respiratory CFD simulations.

DOI: 10.1038/s41598-017-16546-3

Effects of respiratory rate on the fluid mechanics of a reconstructed upper airway

Alister J. Bates, Raouf S. Amin, Ephraim J. Gutmark
Building and Environment • 2021
CFDRespiratory RateFluid Mechanics

Investigation of how respiratory rate affects the fluid mechanics of the upper airway using CFD simulations on reconstructed geometries. This study provides insights into the effects of breathing patterns on airway flow characteristics.

DOI: 10.1016/j.buildenv.2021.108023

The effect of including dynamic imaging derived airway wall motion in CFD simulations of respiratory airflow in patients with OSA

Alister J. Bates, Jason C. Woods, Raouf S. Amin
Scientific Reports • 2024
CFDObstructive Sleep ApneaDynamic Modeling

This study examines how including dynamic airway wall motion affects CFD predictions in patients with obstructive sleep apnea (OSA). The research shows that airway motion significantly impacts pressure loss calculations and clinical assessment of airway resistance.

DOI: 10.1038/s41598-024-68180-6
